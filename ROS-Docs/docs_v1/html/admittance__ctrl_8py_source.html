3 ### A very basic admittance controller to demonstrate the Wrench output and
4 ### cartesian velocity input of the Jaco/Mico node.
5 ### See the associated launch file for running the complete demo.
6 ###
7 ### Defines a deadzone box for the applied forces.
8 ### When the force exceed the deadzone bounds in a dimension, a cartesian
9 ### velocity is applied as a factor (k_f) times the distance from the crossed
10 ### bound (err).
11 ### NOTE: Ignores torque for now.
12 ### NOTE: The wrench output of the Jaco node represents the force that the robot
13 ### applies, and not that it perceives, and is oriented in the base frame.
14 ### For instance, this means that gravity is part of the wrench output as force
15 ### in the positive part of the Z axis.
16 ### This explains the higher upper bound of the deadzone in the Z axis, as this
17 ### deadzone is applied on the raw input, and not the output.
18 ###
19 ### Topics:
20 ### - wrench: Input force (geometry_msgs/StampedWrench).
21 ### - cmd_vel: Cartesian velocity output (geometry_msgs/TwistStamped).
22 ###
23 ### Parameters:
24 ### - dz_x_min: Lower bound of the deadzone in the X axis. Default: -10.0 N.
25 ### - dz_x_max: Upper bound of the deadzone in the X axis. Default: 10.0 N.
26 ### - dz_y_min: Lower bound of the deadzone in the Y axis. Default: -10.0 N.
27 ### - dz_y_max: Upper bound of the deadzone in the Y axis. Default: 10.0 N.
28 ### - dz_z_min: Lower bound of the deadzone in the Z axis. Default: -10.0 N.
29 ### - dz_z_max: Upper bound of the deadzone in the Z axis. Default: 30.0 N.
30 ### - k_f: Response factor (k_f * err). Default: -0.1 m/(s * N).
31 ### - vel_max: Maximum velocity in any dimension. Default: 0.1.
32 ### - period: Update period, in s. Default: 0.10 s (10 Hz)
